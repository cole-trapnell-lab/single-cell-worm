Overview

C. elegans is the only animal for which the entire cellular lineage is known. In Cao et al. (Science, 2017) we profiled the transcriptome of whole C. elegans animals at the L2 stage at single cell resolution using a combinatorial indexing approach (sci-RNA-seq). This essentially provides a "snapshot" of the dynamic molecular state of the worm in development.

Our Analysis

Our analysis in Cao et al. associated cells in the sci-RNA-seq data with 27 high-level cell types as well as several neuronal subtypes. Integration with transcription factor (TF) ChIP-seq data revealed potential regulators of cell type specific gene expression.

Further Experiments

We plan to conduct further sci-RNA-seq experiments aimed at achieving better coverage of rare cell types (especially neurons) and at profiling different stages of development. The data from these experiments will be hosted here. The analysis of these data will be limited however by our ability to conclusively associate sequenced cells with specific anatomical cell types in the worm.

Community Collaboration

We aim to enlist the help of C. elegans community in both improving the annotation of the original Cao et al. data and in annotating data from future experiments. To facilitate this, we will publish data for additional C. elegans sci-RNA-seq experiments online, prior to publication. This website contains documentation on how to access and start performing your own analysis on the Cao et al. data. It also contains instructions on how to access a new dataset (sequenced in July 2017) which profiles gonad cells from young adult C. elegans.
